Herbivores

Herbivores are animals that primarily consume plants. They have specialized teeth for grinding leaves and stems.

  • Elephant: Uses its trunk to reach leaves and branches.
  • Giraffe: Feeds on the leaves of tall trees.
  • Rabbit: Grazes on grass and leafy vegetables.

Carnivores

Carnivores are animals that primarily eat meat. They often have sharp teeth and claws for hunting.

  • Lion: Hunts in groups and preys on large herbivores.
  • Shark: Uses keen senses to locate and catch fish.
  • Eagle: Soars high to spot small mammals from the air.

Omnivores

Omnivores consume both plants and animals. They have a versatile diet.

  • Bear: Forages for berries, nuts, and fish.
  • Human: Eats a wide variety of foods from both categories.
  • Pig: Roots for plants and consumes small animals.
